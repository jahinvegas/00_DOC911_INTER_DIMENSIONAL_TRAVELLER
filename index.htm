Inter-Dimensional Traveller

DOC911 is a traveller, producer, and sound-archivist operating between confirmed reality and the unmapped territories beyond. Trained by no institution, credentialed by no dimension — only by mileage and the scars of re-entry.

Currently stationed at Threshold V. Last confirmed coordinates: 14.228°N / 29.441°Ψ / Δ-7.009.

Chronicles

The Traveller's
Chronicle

Born in the overlap of two dying constellations — one mapped, one not — DOC911 spent the first years of consciousness learning to translate the static between them into something resembling music.

The instruments came later: found at markets with no fixed address, salvaged from dimensions that had already folded. Each one holds a residual frequency — a ghost of the last hand that touched it. DOC911 plays them all simultaneously.

The logbook below is a living record. Not autobiography — more like sonar. Each entry a pulse sent out into the dark, tracked by what echoes back.

Origin

DOC911 does not remember the crossing — only that it happened. A seam between two unstable dimensions, a pressure drop, and then the smell of something burning that had no chemical name. The first sound recorded on the other side was 55Hz, sustained, with a shimmer the instruments could not explain. It became the root note of everything since.

The designation DOC911 came from a filing error in the Lumen District registry. The clerk entered it wrong and refused to correct it. By the time it mattered, it had already become true.

Entry 001 Coord: 14.2°N / 29.4°Ψ
Lumen District
The Market at the Edge of the Map

Found it between a vent stack and a building that had no ground floor — only a second storey hovering three feet above the street, supported by nothing that I could identify. The market below it sold instruments with no names. I bought four. One played itself on the walk back to the transit terminal.

The vendor said the instruments hum because they were tuned inside a collapsing star. I have no way to verify this. What I can verify is that the lowest string of the one I call Number One resonates at exactly 55Hz in any atmospheric pressure — as if it already knows what tuning means and has decided not to change.

Entry 002 Coord: 08.7°S / 41.1°Ψ
Substratum IX
Pulse Cavern — Six Hours Underground

Descended via a shaft cut through what the locals call compressed memory — sedimentary layers of discarded experience, dense as basalt. The cavern at the bottom is bioluminescent, but the light does not behave like light. It pulses in sequences. I spent six hours recording without understanding what I was recording.

Listened back at the surface. The pulses were prime numbers, expressed in light. I don't know what they are counting toward, or whether they have been counting since before there were people in this dimension to notice. I made a track from the rhythm. It felt like theft. I released it anyway.

Entry 003 Coord: 22.3°N / 17.8°Ψ
Dimension V.12
Glass Sea — The Tide That Sings Back

The Glass Sea is not glass and is not a sea. It is a plain of crystallised resonance — solidified standing waves from a sonic event so large it froze the medium it was travelling through. You walk across it and it rings under your feet. Every step is a different pitch, depending on where you place your weight.

I spent three days crossing it. By the second day I had learned to walk in a way that produced chords. By the third I was playing something close to a melody. The photographs do not capture the sound. Nothing does. I tried anyway.

Entry 004 Coord: 31.9°N / 55.2°Ψ
Ruins of Oru
Ashfall Archives — What Survives

Oru collapsed inward approximately 400 years before my arrival. What remains is not ruins exactly — more like an extended exhale. Buildings half-standing, half-dissolved into a fine grey ash that never fully settles. The light is amber, always, because the sun here has a different composition. Everything looks like late afternoon.

Found the photographic plates in a basement that shouldn't have survived. Someone had interleaved them with pages of poetry — the images and the words alternate, so you read the archive like a song. I could not translate the language but the rhythm was clear. I photographed the photographs. The plates crumbled when I touched them. I was the last person to see them. That is a weight I carry.

● Ruins of Oru Photography Archive
Entry 005 Coord: 44.1°N / 62.7°Ψ
Council of Mirrors
Mirror Spires — The Council That Never Speaks

The spires are not made of mirror. They are made of a material that reflects not light but time — each surface shows a different moment in the history of whatever stands before it. I spent an hour watching myself arrive, leave, arrive again, at slightly different angles. The council, if there is one, has never been observed meeting. The spires may be the council.

I recorded fourteen minutes of audio standing between two of them. On playback, the recording contains a third voice that was not present in the room. Low, slow, speaking in a frequency that I can hear but cannot transcribe. I have been playing it back for months. I am beginning to understand the rhythm, if not the words.

● Council of Mirrors Audio Anomaly
Entry 006 Coord: 09.4°N / 78.3°Ψ
Threshold V
Veil Crossing — Between Two Seasons

Threshold V exists at the boundary of two atmospheric systems that should not coexist. On one side it is late autumn — cold, still, the smell of something decaying slowly and without regret. On the other side it is early spring, the specific green that has no name in most languages because it only lasts four days. I stood at the line between them for a long time.

The veil itself is visible as a shimmer — not heat, but something close to it. A thermal of possibility. Animals cross it freely. The locals say it is not a place to linger. I lingered. The photographs I took at the exact boundary show both seasons simultaneously, one on each half of the frame. I did not plan this. The camera understood something I didn't.

● Threshold V Photography
Entry 007 Coord: 52.8°N / 91.0°Ψ
Station 9-L
Starlit Overpass — Transit Above the Suspended City

Station 9-L is a transit hub for dimensions that no longer exist as permanent destinations — they can only be accessed in motion, from the overpass, during the six seconds between departure and arrival. The locals call them ghost dimensions. The transit authority does not acknowledge them on the official maps but sells tickets to them from an unmarked window at the end of Platform 4.

I rode the overpass eleven times. On the eighth pass, for approximately two seconds, I was somewhere else entirely — a city made of light, populated by figures that moved like music sounds, without the separation that bodies usually impose. I did not have time to record it. I am recording it now, from memory, which is the least reliable instrument I own.

● Station 9-L Transit Log Ghost Dimension
Entry 008 Coord: 18.6°S / 33.9°Ψ
Void Market
Silent Bazaar — Trade in What Cannot Be Kept

The Void Market trades in things that cannot be owned: specific qualities of light at a particular moment, the feeling of a word in a language you do not speak, the weight of a decision made by someone else. The stalls are made of what the vendors remember, assembled each morning and disassembled at dusk when the memory fades.

I bought a sound. I do not know how to describe the transaction except that I handed something over and received something back, and both parties seemed satisfied, and I walked away with a frequency in my chest that I had not had before. It is the root note of the track I am currently building. I do not know what I gave in exchange. I notice its absence but cannot name it.
